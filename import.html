Waste in the food industry is characterized by a high ratio of product specific waste not only does this mean that the generation of this waste is unavoidable, but also that the amount and kind of waste product which consists primarily of the organic residue of processed raw materials, can scarcely be altered if the quality of the finished product is to remain consistent. The utilization and disposal of product specific waste is difficult, due to its inadequate biological stability, potentially pathogenic nature, high water content, potential for rapid auto oxidation and high level of enzymic activity. The diverse types of waste generated by various branches of the food industry can be quantified based on the respective level of production.

Waste disposal and by-product management in food processing industry pose problems in the areas of environmental protection and sustainability (Russ and Pittroff 2004). Generally speaking, raw and auxiliary materials, as well as processing acids, enter the production process and exit as one of the following: a desired product, a non-product-specific waste or a product-specific waste. Product-specific waste unavoidably accumulates as a result of processing of raw materials. It is produced during the various steps of production, in which the desired components are extracted from the raw materials. After extraction, there are often other potentially useful components present in the remaining materials.
